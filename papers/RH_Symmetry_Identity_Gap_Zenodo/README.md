## Abstract

This paper presents a structural synthesis of frontier approaches to the Riemann Hypothesis, demonstrating that current mathematics can generate the functional equation's *symmetry* s ↔ 1−s, but lacks the geometric substrate to collapse this symmetry into the *identity* Re(s) = 1/2.

We systematically map the failure points of classical analytic, spectral-theoretic, and modern arithmetic-geometric approaches, showing that all terminate at the requirement for an unproven **Positivity** condition—the "Positivity Bedrock."

We propose a novel theoretical architecture—the *Scholze-Connes hybrid*—combining Connes' non-commutative geometry with Scholze-Clausen's condensed mathematics. We reduce RH to the ampleness of a scaling bundle on a condensed groupoid, and to the semi-stability of a bundle on the Fargues-Fontaine curve.

Despite these sophisticated reformulations, we demonstrate that circularity persists: verifying ampleness or semi-stability requires information equivalent to knowing the zeros.

## Key Findings

### 1. The Positivity Bedrock

Every known approach to RH requires proving a Positivity condition:

| Approach | Positivity Form |
|----------|-----------------|
| Connes NCG | Weil inner product |
| Theory of Motives | Standard Conjecture D |
| SUSY QM | H = {Q,Q†} ≥ 0 |
| Fargues-Fontaine | Semi-stability of slope 0 |

All are equivalent to RH—creating circularity.

### 2. The Circularity Theorem

Every attempt to verify ampleness or semi-stability encounters a step equivalent to knowing the zeros.

### 3. Required Future Mathematics
