1.1 Axioms and Definitions

We begin by establishing the axiomatic foundation of the Z² framework. The framework rests on three pillars of established physics, from which all else follows.

Axiom 1 (Einstein Field Equations)
The geometry of spacetime is determined by its matter-energy content through: 
G_μν = 8πG T_μν
where the coefficient 8π is derived from requiring consistency with Newtonian gravity in the weak-field limit.
Axiom 2 (Friedmann Equation)
For a homogeneous, isotropic universe described by the FLRW metric, the Hubble parameter satisfies: 
H² = (8πG/3)ρ
This follows from Axiom 1 applied to the FLRW metric. The factor 3 arises from the trace of the spatial metric.
Axiom 3 (Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy)
A black hole of horizon area A carries entropy: 
S = A/(4ℓ_P²) = A/(4Għ/c³)
The factor 4 is derived from Hawking's calculation of black hole temperature T = ħκ/(2πc), where κ is the surface gravity.

Z ≡ 2√(8π/3)

Z² = 4 × (8π/3) = 32π/3 ≈ 33.510321638

1.2 Derivation of Z from First Principles

Theorem 1.1 (Derivation of Z)
The constant Z = 2√(8π/3) emerges necessarily from the combination of horizon physics and cosmological dynamics, with all numerical factors derived from Axioms 1-3.
Proof.
Step 1: Cosmological Horizon Properties Consider the Hubble sphere of radius r_H = c/H. The mass contained within this sphere, assuming critical density ρ_c = 3H²/(8πG), is: 
M_H = ρ_c × (4π/3)r_H³
    = [3H²/(8πG)] × (4π/3)(c/H)³
    = c³/(2GH)
The Schwarzschild radius of this mass is: 
r_S = 2GM_H/c² = 2G[c³/(2GH)]/c² = c/H = r_H
Thus the Hubble sphere is its own Schwarzschild radius—a remarkable self-consistency. Step 2: Gravitational Acceleration at the Horizon The Newtonian gravitational acceleration at r_H is: 
g_H = GM_H/r_H² = G[c³/(2GH)]/(c/H)² = cH/2
The factor 2 arises from the Schwarzschild mass formula, not as an assumption. Step 3: Cosmological Screening The Friedmann equation couples local physics to cosmic expansion through the factor 8π/3. For accelerations measured against the cosmological background, we have a screening factor: 
a_cosmo = a_local / √(8π/3)
At the horizon, where a_local = g_H: 
a₀ = g_H / √(8π/3) = (cH/2) / √(8π/3)
Step 4: The Zimmerman Constant The dimensionless ratio of cosmic acceleration cH to the screened acceleration a₀ defines Z: 
Z ≡ cH/a₀ = cH / [(cH/2)/√(8π/3)]
  = 2√(8π/3)
Therefore: 
Z² = 4 × (8π/3) = 32π/3
All numerical factors are derived: • 4 from Bekenstein-Hawking entropy (Axiom 3) • 8π from Einstein equations (Axiom 1) • 3 from spatial dimensions in FLRW (Axiom 2) • 2 from Schwarzschild horizon physics
