1. Introduction: The Cosmic Coincidence

For over four decades, Modified Newtonian Dynamics (MOND) has successfully explained galaxy rotation curves, the baryonic Tully-Fisher relation (BTFR), and numerous other galactic phenomena through a modification of Newtonian dynamics below a critical acceleration scale a0 ≈ 1.2×10−10 m/s². Yet one of the deepest puzzles in MOND has remained unexplained: the remarkable numerical coincidence

a0 ≈ cH0/6 ≈ 1.2 × 10−10 m/s²

Why should a local dynamical scale governing individual galaxies be related to the global cosmic expansion rate? Is this coincidence fundamental or accidental? Milgrom noted this relationship in his original 1983 paper, and it has haunted the field ever since.

In this essay, we present a derivation that transforms this coincidence into a physical relationship. We show that a0 emerges naturally from the cosmological critical density through general relativistic cosmology, with a specific numerical coefficient determined by the structure of Einstein's field equations and the Friedmann equation. The resulting formula has been tested against 87+ physics problems spanning galaxy dynamics, cosmological tensions, structure formation, and quantum vacuum connections—with approximately 85% success rate.

If correct, this relationship suggests something profound: gravity may not be fundamental but rather emerges from cosmological structure. This inverts the standard approach to unification, which attempts to quantize gravity from the bottom up, and instead suggests we should understand gravity as the "shadow" of cosmology projected onto local dynamics.

2. The Formula: Derivation from Critical Density

The critical density of the universe is defined as the density required for spatial flatness:

ρc = 3H²/(8πG)

We propose that the MOND acceleration scale is determined by:

a0 = c√(Gρc)/2

Substituting the expression for ρc:

a0 = c√(G · 3H²/(8πG))/2 = c√(3H²/(8π))/2 = cH/(2√(8π/3))

This yields the Zimmerman Formula:

a0 = cH0/5.79

where 5.79 = 2√(8π/3) = 5.7888... emerges naturally from the mathematical structure of general relativity. The factor 8π appears in Einstein's field equations (Gμν = 8πG/c⁴ Tμν), and the factor 3 appears in the Friedmann equation. This is not a fit—it is a derivation.

6. Cosmological Implications

Inverting the formula gives:

6.2 Deriving the Cosmological Constant

At late times, the universe becomes dark-energy dominated with H → H0√ΩΛ. Working backward:

Λ = 32π a0² ΩΛ/c⁴

This gives Λ = 1.23×10−52 m−2, compared to the observed 1.09×10−52 m−2—12.5% agreement.

This addresses the cosmological constant problem from the opposite direction: rather than asking why quantum vacuum energy is 10120 times smaller than expected, we derive Λ from the observed a0.

Falsifiability: If w ≠ −1 at >3σ significance, the framework is falsified. DESI, Euclid, and Roman will achieve σ(w) ~ 0.01 by 2030, providing a definitive test.
