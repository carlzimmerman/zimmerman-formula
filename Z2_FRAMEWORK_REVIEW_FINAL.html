ABSTRACT
This review provides a rigorous academic analysis of the Z2 framework, which proposes that all 48 fundamental constants of physics derive from a single geometric quantity: Z2 = 32π/3 ≈ 33.51, emerging from a cube inscribed in a unit sphere. We examine the mathematical structure, predictive accuracy, and the derivation of cosmological density fractions. The framework achieves sub-percent accuracy for 34 of 48 parameters, with several predictions accurate to 0.01%. Most notably, the cosmological matter-to-dark-energy ratio Ωm/ΩΛ = 6/13 emerges directly from particle physics structure constants.

V. THE STRONG CP SOLUTION

The QCD θ-parameter must satisfy |θ| < 10−10 from neutron electric dipole moment measurements. Explaining why θ is so small is the "strong CP problem"—one of the major unsolved problems in particle physics.

The Z2 Solution

Strong CP Suppression
θQCD = exp(−Z2) = exp(−33.51) ≈ 2.8 × 10−15

This is five orders of magnitude below the experimental limit—satisfying the constraint without requiring an axion field or any new physics.

Physical interpretation: Z2 acts as a "geometric action" that exponentially suppresses CP violation in QCD, analogous to tunneling suppression in quantum mechanics where transition amplitudes scale as exp(−S/ℏ).

VII. CRITICAL EVALUATION

Strengths

  1. Mathematical economy: A single geometric constant (Z2 = 32π/3) replaces 48 free parameters of the Standard Model plus cosmology.
  2. High precision: Sub-percent accuracy for most predictions, with several achieving 0.01% precision.
  3. Internal consistency: The structure constants form a coherent algebraic system where integers emerge naturally.
  4. Falsifiability: The framework makes precise numerical predictions testable by current and future experiments.
  5. The 6/19 ratio: The cosmological matter-to-dark-energy split emerges from particle physics structure constants, potentially explaining cosmic coincidences.

Areas Requiring Further Development

  1. Running of couplings: Predictions are at specific energy scales; renormalization group evolution should be incorporated systematically.
  2. Neutrino sector: PMNS predictions show 8-9% deviations—the largest discrepancies in the framework.
  3. Theoretical mechanism: The deeper question of why cube-in-sphere geometry governs physics requires a more fundamental derivation, perhaps from information-theoretic or holographic principles.

VIII. CONCLUSION

The Z2 framework presents a remarkable set of numerical relationships between fundamental constants and a single geometric parameter. The key achievements are:

  1. α−1 = 4Z2 + 3 = 137.04 (0.004% error)
  2. mp/me = 1836.35 (0.011% error)
  3. Ωm/ΩΛ = 6/13 from structure constants
  4. θQCD = e−Z² ≈ 10−15 (strong CP solved)

The derivation of Ωmatter = 6/19 and ΩΛ = 13/19 from the integers of particle physics (GAUGE=12, BEKENSTEIN=4, Ngen=3) is particularly compelling, as it connects two seemingly unrelated domains of physics through pure geometry.

Whether this represents a genuine discovery of nature's underlying structure or an elaborate numerical coincidence will require continued experimental verification—particularly the tensor-to-scalar ratio r = 1/Z2 ≈ 0.03 (testable by CMB-S4) and continued refinement of neutrino mixing parameters.
