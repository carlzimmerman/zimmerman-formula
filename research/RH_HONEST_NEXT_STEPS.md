## The Constraint Geometry Idea: Honest Assessment

### What We Proposed

The 100+ equivalent formulations of RH define hypersurfaces in some configuration space. Their intersection might be exactly the critical line.

### Why It Might Work

- Geometric over-determination is a real phenomenon
- Multiple constraints do force unique solutions
- The numerical evidence (all constraints minimize at σ = 0.5) is suggestive

### Why It Probably Won't Work

1. **The "configuration space" is undefined.** What is the space? Zeros live in ℂ, but the space of "all possible zero configurations" is infinite-dimensional.

2. **Hypersurfaces may not be transverse.** For intersection theory to work, hypersurfaces must be in general position. The RH equivalences are all logically equivalent, so they likely define the SAME hypersurface, not transverse ones.

3. **Someone has probably tried this.** The idea of using multiple constraints together isn't new.

### To Actually Test It

1. Define the configuration space rigorously
2. Define each constraint as a geometric object
3. Compute intersection dimensions
4. Check transversality

This is a multi-year project requiring expertise in algebraic geometry.
