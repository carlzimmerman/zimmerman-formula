## TIER 1: STRONG CONNECTION (Examples to Create)

### 1. Lina Necib - Galactic Dynamics & Dark Matter
**Research:** Uses stellar streams and halo kinematics to study dark matter distribution
**Zimmerman Connection:** MOND with a₀ = 1.2×10⁻¹⁰ predicts NO dark matter halo - stellar dynamics from modified gravity
**Testable:** Velocity dispersion of halo stars at r > 8 kpc should follow MOND, not NFW profile
**Example:** 11_milky_way_dynamics

### 2. Mikhail Ivanov - Large-Scale Structure
**Research:** LSS, effective field theories, cosmological data analysis
**Zimmerman Connection:** Evolving a₀ directly affects matter power spectrum and structure growth rate
**Testable:** S8 tension, growth rate f(z)σ8, BAO + RSD combinations
**Example:** Already covered by 09_s8_tension, can extend

### 3. Mark Vogelsberger - Structure Formation Simulations
**Research:** IllustrisTNG, galaxy formation, dark matter physics
**Zimmerman Connection:** MOND + evolving a₀ would produce faster structure formation at high-z
**Testable:** Halo mass function, galaxy stellar mass function evolution
**Example:** 12_structure_formation

### 4. Paul Schechter - Strong Gravitational Lensing
**Research:** 4-image quasar lenses to measure H₀ and test gravity
**Zimmerman Connection:** MOND predicts different lensing convergence; evolving a₀ affects high-z lenses
**Testable:** Time delay distances, flux ratios, substructure
**Example:** 13_strong_lensing

### 5. Robert Simcoe - Early Galaxies & Spectroscopy
**Research:** First stars/galaxies, metal abundances, reionization
**Zimmerman Connection:** Higher a₀ at z>6 means faster galaxy formation, different IMF effects
**Testable:** Metal enrichment timescales, early galaxy masses
**Example:** Similar to 02_jwst_highz_test
