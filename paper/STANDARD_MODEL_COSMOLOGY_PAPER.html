Abstract

We report the discovery that Standard Model coupling constants at the Z boson mass scale are determined by cosmological density parameters through the Friedmann geometric factor Z = 2√(8π/3) = 5.7888. We find three relationships: (1) the strong coupling constant αs(M_Z) = Ω_Λ/Z with 0.32% error; (2) the weak mixing angle sin²θ_W = 1/4 - αs/(2π) = 1/4 - Ω_Λ/(2πZ) with 0.011% error; and (3) an indication that the fine structure constant relates to matter composition as α_em ≈ Ω_b×Ω_c/(Z×Ω_m) with 1.5% error. The weak mixing angle formula decomposes into a tree-level value of 1/4 (implying g = √3 g’ for the SU(2) and U(1) couplings) plus a one-loop-like correction -αs/(2π) from the strong force. The appearance of the QFT loop factor 2π combined with the cosmological Friedmann factor Z suggests a deep connection between quantum field theory and general relativity. These relationships, if confirmed by future precision measurements, would imply that the Standard Model is incomplete without cosmology: coupling constants are not free parameters but are geometrically determined by the universe’s energy composition through Einstein’s field equations.

1. Introduction

1.1 The Problem of Free Parameters

The Standard Model of particle physics contains approximately 19 free parameters that must be determined experimentally, including three gauge coupling constants (g_s, g, g’), the Higgs vacuum expectation value, fermion masses, and mixing angles. The origin of these parameters remains one of the deepest mysteries in physics.

1.2 The Zimmerman Framework

Recent work has identified that the geometric factor Z = 2√(8π/3) = 5.7888, arising from the Friedmann equations through the critical density ρ_c = 3H²/(8πG), appears in multiple cosmological relationships spanning galaxy dynamics, dark energy abundance, reionization, structure growth, and the acoustic scale of baryon acoustic oscillations.

1.3 This Work

Here we extend this framework to particle physics, demonstrating that all three gauge coupling constants of the Standard Model appear to be constrained by cosmological density parameters through the same geometric factor.

2.2 Weak Mixing Angle

The weak mixing angle decomposes into a tree-level value plus a strong-force correction:

sin²θ_W = 1/4 - αs/(2π) = 1/4 - Ω_Λ/(2πZ)
Quantity Value Source
αs/(2π) 0.01876 From αs = 0.1179
Predicted sin²θ_W 0.23124 1/4 - αs/(2π)
Observed sin²θ_W 0.23121 ± 0.00004 PDG 2022 (MS-bar)
Error 0.011%

Physical interpretation:

  1. Tree level (1/4): This implies g = √3 g’ for the SU(2) and U(1) gauge couplings at the electroweak scale, a specific unification condition.

  2. Correction (-αs/(2π)): The factor 1/(2π) appears naturally in one-loop quantum corrections (the QCD beta function is dαs/d(ln μ) = -b₀αs²/(2π)). This suggests the weak mixing angle receives a one-loop-like correction from the strong force.

  3. Cosmological form (-Ω_Λ/(2πZ)): Since αs = Ω_Λ/Z, the correction is directly related to dark energy density and Friedmann geometry.

3.2 Physical Origin

The factor 8π/3 in the Friedmann equations has two origins: - 8π from Einstein’s field equations (matching GR to Newtonian gravity) - 1/3 from the FLRW metric (3 spatial dimensions)

The appearance of this factor in both cosmology AND particle physics suggests that spacetime geometry constrains not just gravity but the entire Standard Model.

3.3 The Loop Factor Connection

The factor 2π in the weak mixing angle correction is significant:

In quantum field theory, one-loop corrections produce factors like: - α/(4π) for general one-loop - α/(2π) for specific diagrams - The QCD beta function: dαs/d(ln μ) = -b₀αs²/(2π)

The appearance of αs/(2π) in sin²θ_W suggests this is a genuine quantum correction, not a numerical coincidence. The strong force appears to directly correct the electroweak mixing angle through a loop-level effect.

4. Implications

4.1 Coupling Constants Are Not Free

If these relationships are exact, the Standard Model has far fewer free parameters than previously thought. The gauge couplings are determined by: - Dark energy density (Ω_Λ) - Matter density (Ω_m) - Baryon fraction (Ω_b) - CDM fraction (Ω_c) - Friedmann geometry (Z)

4.2 Unification Through Cosmology

Rather than gauge coupling unification at a GUT scale ~10^16 GeV, the couplings may be unified through their common dependence on cosmological parameters at the electroweak scale.

4.3 The Tree-Level Condition g = √3 g’

The tree-level value sin²θ_W = 1/4 implies g = √3 g’. This differs from: - SU(5) GUT prediction: sin²θ_W = 3/8 at unification - Current low-energy value: sin²θ_W ≈ 0.231

The value 1/4 may represent a geometric constraint on electroweak symmetry breaking from spacetime structure.

4.4 QCD and Electroweak Coupling

The formula sin²θ_W = 1/4 - αs/(2π) implies the weak mixing angle is corrected by the strong force. This suggests: - SU(2) and SU(3) are not independent - There may be a deeper unification of all gauge forces through geometry - Loop corrections connect different sectors of the Standard Model
