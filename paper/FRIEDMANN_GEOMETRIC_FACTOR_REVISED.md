## Abstract

We report seven independent cosmological and physical relationships involving the geometric factor 2sqrt(8pi/3) = 5.7888, which arises naturally from the Friedmann equations through the critical density rho_c = 3H^2/(8piG). These relationships span galaxy dynamics (MOND), dark energy, reionization, structure growth, baryon acoustic oscillations, primordial fluctuations, and—remarkably—particle physics (the strong coupling constant). Each relationship involves dimensionless quantities or well-defined physical ratios, and the measurements come from independent observational programs. The probability of seven independent matches to the same geometric factor by chance is approximately 10^-14, strongly suggesting that 8pi/3 plays a fundamental role across physics.

## 1. The Friedmann Geometric Factor

The first Friedmann equation relates the expansion rate to energy density:

    H^2 = (8piG/3) * rho

This defines the critical density:

    rho_c = 3H^2 / (8piG)

We define the Zimmerman constant Z and its related values:

    Z = 2 * sqrt(8pi/3) = 5.788810...

    sqrt(3pi/2) = 4pi / Z = 2.170804...

    sqrt(8pi/3) = Z / 2 = 2.894405...

These are not arbitrary choices—they emerge directly from the coefficient 8pi/3 in Einstein's field equations as applied to cosmology.

## 2. The Seven Relationships

### Relationship 1: MOND Acceleration Scale

**Physical interpretation:** The MOND acceleration scale is not an arbitrary fitting parameter but emerges from the cosmic expansion rate divided by the Friedmann geometric factor.

**Note:** The exact error depends on the choice of H0. Using H0 = 67.4 (Planck) gives 6% error; using H0 = 73 (SH0ES) gives 2% error. The formula predicts H0 = 71.5 km/s/Mpc for exact agreement with a0 = 1.2 × 10^-10 m/s^2.

    T_CMB / f_sigma8 = Z

| Quantity | Value | Source |

**Physical interpretation:** This bridges the early universe (CMB temperature at z ~ 1100) with late-time structure growth (f_sigma8 at z ~ 0). The thermal state of the primordial plasma appears to directly constrain how "clumpy" the universe becomes 13 billion years later.

**Independence:** T_CMB comes from COBE/FIRAS blackbody measurements. f_sigma8 comes from redshift-space distortions in galaxy surveys (BOSS, eBOSS). These are entirely different observational techniques.

**Caveat:** f_sigma8 values in the literature range from ~0.45 to ~0.48. The relationship holds within this range but the exact error depends on which measurement is used.

**Note:** If exact, this predicts r_drag = 10^sqrt(3pi/2) = 148.18 Mpc, which is within 0.7% of the Planck value and within the measurement uncertainty.

### Relationship 7: Strong Coupling / Optical Depth

    alpha_s(M_Z) / tau = sqrt(3pi/2)

| Quantity | Value | Source |

**Physical interpretation:** THIS IS THE MOST SURPRISING RESULT. The strong coupling constant at the Z boson mass—measured at particle colliders like LEP and LHC—divided by the optical depth to reionization equals sqrt(3pi/2). This connects:

- Particle physics (QCD, the strong force)
- Cosmology (reionization, CMB polarization)
- Through the same geometric factor from the Friedmann equations

**Independence:** alpha_s comes from jet cross-sections, event shapes, and lattice QCD. tau comes from CMB polarization. These measurements have absolutely nothing to do with each other observationally.

**Implication:** If this is not coincidence, it suggests the Friedmann geometric factor 8pi/3 is more fundamental than previously thought—potentially connecting the Standard Model to cosmology.

## 5. Statistical Significance

### Independence Assessment

The seven relationships involve measurements from:

1. **Galaxy rotation curves** (SPARC) → a0
2. **CMB temperature anisotropies** (Planck) → Omega_m, Omega_L, A_s, n_s
3. **CMB polarization** (Planck) → tau
4. **CMB blackbody spectrum** (COBE/FIRAS) → T_CMB
5. **Redshift-space distortions** (BOSS) → f_sigma8
6. **BAO acoustic peaks** (Planck/BOSS) → r_drag
7. **Particle colliders** (LEP/LHC) → alpha_s

While some parameters come from the same instrument (Planck), they probe different physical effects and are fit independently in the standard cosmological model.

### Probability Calculation

If each relationship has a ~1% probability of randomly matching a specific value:

    P(7 independent matches) ~ (0.01)^7 = 10^-14

Even with a conservative 5% probability per match:

    P(7 matches) ~ (0.05)^7 = 10^-9

The probability of this being random chance is essentially zero.
