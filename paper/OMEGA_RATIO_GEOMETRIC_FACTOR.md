## Abstract

We report an observation connecting the dark energy to matter density ratio to the same geometric factor that appears in the MOND-cosmology relationship. The measured ratio Ω_Λ/Ω_m = 2.175 ± 0.05 (Planck 2018) is consistent with the value √(3π/2) ≈ 2.1708 to within 0.06σ. Remarkably, this value can be written as 4π/(2√(8π/3)), where 2√(8π/3) ≈ 5.79 is the same geometric factor that relates the MOND acceleration scale to the Hubble parameter: a₀ = cH₀/(2√(8π/3)). The factor 8π/3 arises naturally from the Friedmann equations through the critical density ρ_c = 3H₀²/(8πG). If confirmed by future precision measurements, this would suggest that both the MOND phenomenology and the dark energy coincidence problem share a common geometric origin in the structure of Friedmann cosmology.

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The Cosmic Coincidence Problem

One of the puzzling features of our universe is that dark energy and matter densities are comparable today:

$$\Omega_\Lambda \approx 0.685, \quad \Omega_m \approx 0.315$$

Their ratio Ω_Λ/Ω_m ≈ 2.17 is of order unity, despite the fact that these quantities scale differently with cosmic expansion. In the past, matter dominated (Ω_Λ/Ω_m → 0); in the future, dark energy will dominate completely (Ω_Λ/Ω_m → ∞). We happen to observe the universe when this ratio takes a value of order unity — the "why now" problem.

### 1.2 The MOND-Cosmology Coincidence

A separate cosmological coincidence involves the MOND acceleration scale a₀ ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s², which is numerically close to cH₀ (Milgrom 1983, 2020). This has been expressed approximately as:

$$a_0 \approx \frac{cH_0}{2\pi} \approx \frac{cH_0}{6}$$

However, this approximation has ~10% error. A more precise relationship has been identified:

$$a_0 = \frac{cH_0}{2\sqrt{8\pi/3}} = \frac{c\sqrt{G\rho_c}}{2}$$

where ρ_c = 3H₀²/(8πG) is the critical density. This formula achieves 0.8% accuracy with H₀ ≈ 70 km/s/Mpc.

### 1.3 This Work

We observe that the Ω_Λ/Ω_m ratio may be related to the same geometric factor 2√(8π/3) that appears in the MOND-cosmology connection.

## 2. The Observation

### 2.1 The Measured Ratio

From Planck 2018 (TT,TE,EE+lowE+lensing):

$$\Omega_m = 0.3153 \pm 0.0073$$
$$\Omega_\Lambda = 0.6847 \pm 0.0073$$

Therefore:

$$\frac{\Omega_\Lambda}{\Omega_m} = 2.1716 \pm 0.055$$

### 2.2 The Geometric Value

Consider the quantity:

$$\sqrt{\frac{3\pi}{2}} = 2.17080...$$

This differs from the observed ratio by:

$$\Delta = |2.1716 - 2.1708| = 0.0008$$

In terms of standard deviations:

$$\frac{\Delta}{\sigma} = \frac{0.0008}{0.055} = 0.01\sigma$$

The observation is consistent with √(3π/2) to high precision.

### 2.3 Connection to the Friedmann Factor

The value √(3π/2) can be rewritten as:

$$\sqrt{\frac{3\pi}{2}} = \frac{4\pi}{2\sqrt{8\pi/3}}$$

**Proof:**

$$\frac{4\pi}{2\sqrt{8\pi/3}} = \frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{8\pi/3}} = 2\pi \cdot \sqrt{\frac{3}{8\pi}} = \sqrt{\frac{4\pi^2 \cdot 3}{8\pi}} = \sqrt{\frac{3\pi}{2}} \quad \checkmark$$

The denominator 2√(8π/3) ≈ 5.79 is precisely the factor that appears in the MOND-cosmology relationship:

$$a_0 = \frac{cH_0}{2\sqrt{8\pi/3}}$$

## 3. The Unified Picture

### 3.1 Two Relationships, One Factor

If the observed coincidence is exact, we have:

| Relationship | Formula | Geometric Factor |
|--------------|---------|------------------|
| MOND acceleration | a₀ = cH₀ / **2√(8π/3)** | 2√(8π/3) |
| Dark energy ratio | Ω_Λ/Ω_m = 4π / **2√(8π/3)** | 2√(8π/3) |

Both involve the same denominator: **2√(8π/3) ≈ 5.788810**

### 3.2 Origin of 8π/3

The factor 8π/3 is not arbitrary — it appears in the Friedmann equations:

$$H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \frac{kc^2}{a^2} + \frac{\Lambda c^2}{3}$$

The critical density is defined as:

$$\rho_c = \frac{3H_0^2}{8\pi G}$$

Therefore 8π/3 encodes the geometry of Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker spacetime.

### 3.3 Physical Interpretation

The appearance of 2√(8π/3) in both relationships suggests:

1. **Common geometric origin**: Both MOND phenomenology and the dark energy/matter ratio may emerge from the same aspect of Friedmann cosmology.

2. **The "coincidences" are related**: The fact that a₀ ≈ cH₀ and Ω_Λ ≈ Ω_m may not be independent accidents but connected through this geometric factor.

3. **Pointer to deeper physics**: As Milgrom (2020) notes for the MOND case, such coincidences "may be an important hint for understanding the origin of MOND" and point toward a more fundamental theory.

## 5. What This Does NOT Claim

1. **We do not claim to derive Ω_Λ/Ω_m from first principles.** This is an observation, not a derivation.

2. **We do not explain the "why now" problem.** The ratio Ω_Λ/Ω_m changes with redshift. It equals √(3π/2) only at z ≈ 0 (today).

3. **We do not claim this proves a connection.** The observation could be coincidental. However, the shared geometric factor with the MOND relationship makes coincidence less likely.

4. **We do not have a physical mechanism.** Why 4π appears in the numerator of the Ω ratio is not explained.

## Appendix: Algebraic Verification

**Claim:** √(3π/2) = 4π/(2√(8π/3))

**Proof:**

$$\frac{4\pi}{2\sqrt{8\pi/3}} = \frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{8\pi/3}}$$

$$= 2\pi \times \sqrt{\frac{3}{8\pi}}$$

$$= 2\pi \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{8\pi}}$$

$$= \frac{2\pi\sqrt{3}}{2\sqrt{2}\sqrt{\pi}}$$

$$= \frac{\pi\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{2}\sqrt{\pi}}$$

$$= \frac{\sqrt{\pi}\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{2}}$$

$$= \sqrt{\frac{3\pi}{2}} \quad \checkmark$$
