The Zimmerman Formula

Derivation and Testable Predictions
Carl Zimmerman
March 2026
DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19114050
Abstract: We present a derivation relating the MOND acceleration scale a₀ to cosmological parameters. From the Friedmann critical density, we derive a₀ = c√(Gρc)/2 = cH₀/5.79, where 5.79 = 2√(8π/3) emerges from the Friedmann equation structure. This achieves 0.5% agreement with observed a₀ and predicts that a₀(z) = a₀(0) × E(z). This paper presents the mathematical derivation, its direct consequences, and quantitative predictions that can confirm or refute the formula.

1. The Derivation

1.1 Starting Point: Friedmann Critical Density

The critical density for a flat universe:

\[ \rho_c = \frac{3 H_0^2}{8\pi G} \]

1.2 Constructing an Acceleration Scale

Using dimensional analysis with c, G, and ρc to construct an acceleration [L T⁻²]:

\[ [c \sqrt{G \rho_c}] = \frac{L}{T} \times \sqrt{\frac{L^3}{M T^2} \times \frac{M}{L^3}} = \frac{L}{T} \times \frac{1}{T} = \frac{L}{T^2} \] ✓

1.3 Determining the Coefficient

Substituting ρc = 3H₀²/(8πG):

\[ a_0 = \frac{c}{\alpha} \sqrt{\frac{3 H_0^2}{8\pi}} = \frac{c H_0}{\alpha \sqrt{8\pi/3}} \]

Setting α = 2:

\[ \boxed{a_0 = \frac{c H_0}{5.79}} \quad \text{where } 5.79 = 2\sqrt{\frac{8\pi}{3}} \]

2.3 The Cosmic Coincidence Resolved

The observation a₀ ≈ cH₀ is not coincidental:

\[ \frac{a_0}{c H_0} = \frac{1}{5.79} = 0.173 \]

The factor 5.79 = 2√(8π/3) arises from the Friedmann equation geometry.

5. What This Does NOT Prove

  1. Does not prove MOND is correct — only that IF MOND is correct, a₀ is determined by cosmology
  2. Does not explain the Bullet Cluster — lensing/gas offset remains unexplained
  3. Does not derive the interpolating function — only the scale a₀
  4. Does not prove dark matter doesn't exist — provides alternative scaling
