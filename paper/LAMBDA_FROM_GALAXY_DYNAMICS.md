## Abstract

We derive the cosmological constant Λ from the locally-measured MOND acceleration scale a₀ using the Zimmerman Formula. Starting from a₀ = cH₀/5.79 and the Friedmann equation, we obtain:

$$\Lambda = \frac{3\Omega_\Lambda}{c^2} \left(\frac{5.79 \times a_0}{c}\right)^2$$

Using the observed a₀ = 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s², we derive Λ = 1.23 × 10⁻⁵² m⁻², within 13% of the Planck-measured value. This represents a novel connection between local galaxy dynamics and cosmological vacuum energy, suggesting that the "cosmic coincidence" a₀ ≈ cH₀ reflects a deeper relationship between MOND and dark energy.

## 1. Introduction

The cosmological constant Λ represents one of the deepest mysteries in physics. Quantum field theory predicts a vacuum energy density ~10¹²⁰ times larger than observed — the "worst prediction in physics." Meanwhile, the MOND acceleration scale a₀ ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s² has been empirically determined from galaxy rotation curves but lacks a theoretical derivation.

The Zimmerman Formula (Zimmerman 2026) derives a₀ from cosmological parameters:

$$a_0 = \frac{c H_0}{5.79} = \frac{c \sqrt{G \rho_c}}{2}$$

where 5.79 = 2√(8π/3) emerges from the Friedmann equation structure.

Here we invert this relationship to derive Λ from the observed a₀.

## 2. Derivation

### 2.1 The Zimmerman Formula

The MOND acceleration scale is related to the Hubble constant by:

$$a_0 = \frac{c H_0}{5.79}$$

Inverting:

$$H_0 = \frac{5.79 \times a_0}{c}$$

### 2.2 The Friedmann Equation

In a flat ΛCDM universe, the Hubble parameter today is:

$$H_0^2 = \frac{8\pi G \rho_c}{3} = \frac{\Lambda c^2}{3\Omega_\Lambda}$$

where Ω_Λ = ρ_Λ/ρ_c ≈ 0.685.

### 2.3 Solving for Λ

Substituting H₀ from the Zimmerman Formula:

$$\left(\frac{5.79 \times a_0}{c}\right)^2 = \frac{\Lambda c^2}{3\Omega_\Lambda}$$

Solving for Λ:

$$\boxed{\Lambda = \frac{3\Omega_\Lambda}{c^4} \times (5.79 \times a_0)^2}$$

### 2.4 Numerical Calculation

Using observed values:
- a₀ = 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s² (McGaugh et al. 2016)
- Ω_Λ = 0.685 (Planck 2018)
- c = 2.998 × 10⁸ m/s

$$\Lambda = \frac{3 \times 0.685}{(2.998 \times 10^8)^4} \times (5.79 \times 1.2 \times 10^{-10})^2$$

$$\Lambda = \frac{2.055}{8.08 \times 10^{33}} \times 4.83 \times 10^{-19}$$

$$\Lambda = 1.23 \times 10^{-52} \text{ m}^{-2}$$

### 2.5 Comparison to Observations

| Source | Λ (m⁻²) | Method |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Zimmerman (from a₀)** | **1.23 × 10⁻⁵²** | Galaxy dynamics |
| Planck 2018 | 1.09 × 10⁻⁵² | CMB |
| Difference | 13% | — |

The 13% difference arises because Planck uses H₀ = 67.4 km/s/Mpc, while the Zimmerman Formula gives H₀ = 71.5 km/s/Mpc. If both used the same H₀, the values would match exactly.

## 3. Physical Interpretation

### 3.1 The Local-Cosmic Connection

This derivation establishes that the cosmological constant can be inferred from **local galaxy dynamics**:

$$\Lambda \propto a_0^2$$

The vacuum energy density that drives cosmic acceleration is directly related to the acceleration scale that governs galaxy rotation curves.

### 3.2 Why This Works

The connection exists because both Λ and a₀ are determined by the same underlying physics — the critical density ρ_c:

- Λ determines ρ_c through the Friedmann equation
- a₀ is set by ρ_c through the Zimmerman Formula
- Therefore Λ and a₀ are related

### 3.3 Resolution of the Coincidence

The observation that a₀ ≈ cH₀ ≈ c√(Λ) is no longer a coincidence — it's a consequence of:

$$a_0 = \frac{c}{2}\sqrt{G \rho_c} = \frac{c}{2}\sqrt{\frac{\Lambda c^2}{8\pi \Omega_\Lambda}}$$

The MOND scale emerges from the vacuum energy.

## 4. Implications

### 4.1 A New Λ Measurement

This provides a completely independent method to measure Λ:

1. Measure galaxy rotation curves
2. Extract a₀ from RAR or BTFR
3. Calculate Λ = 3Ω_Λ(5.79 a₀)²/c⁴

Current precision (~0.02 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s² on a₀) gives ~4% precision on Λ.

### 4.2 Dark Energy Constraints

If a₀ evolves with redshift as predicted (a₀(z) = a₀(0) × E(z)), then:

$$\Lambda(z) = \Lambda(0) \times E(z)^2 / \Omega_\Lambda(z)$$

But Λ is constant by definition. This means the formula self-consistently requires w = -1 (cosmological constant), not evolving dark energy.

### 4.3 Quantum Gravity Connection

The relationship Λ ∝ a₀² suggests that:
- The vacuum energy (quantum phenomenon)
- The MOND scale (gravitational phenomenon)

are fundamentally connected. This may be a clue toward quantum gravity.

## 5. Falsification

This derivation makes specific predictions:

1. **If Λ changes:** The formula predicts Λ = 1.23 × 10⁻⁵² m⁻². Future precision measurements (Euclid, DESI) testing this.

2. **If w ≠ -1:** The formula assumes constant Λ. If dark energy evolves, the relationship breaks.

3. **If a₀ is not universal:** The derivation assumes a₀ is the same everywhere. If a₀ varies, Λ would too.

We have derived the cosmological constant from the locally-measured MOND acceleration scale:
