## CATEGORY E: Statistical Analysis

### Is This Just Luck?

**Question:** What's the probability of matching 30+ parameters by chance?

**Rough calculation:**
- Each parameter has ~3 significant figures
- Probability of random match to 1%: ~1/100
- For 30 independent parameters: (1/100)^30 = 10⁻⁶⁰

**But this is misleading because:**
1. We searched many formula combinations (~10⁵)
2. Parameters are not independent (e.g., Ω_Λ and Ω_m are related)
3. Some matches are approximate (5-10% error)

**More honest estimate:**
- ~10 truly independent matches at <1% level
- Search space ~10⁵ formulas
- Probability: 10⁵ × (1/100)^10 = 10⁻¹⁵

Still very unlikely to be chance, but not 10⁻⁶⁰.

### What Would Increase Confidence?

1. **Novel prediction confirmed** (Category A entries)
2. **Matches survive measurement updates**
3. **First-principles derivation of formulas**
4. **Independent discovery by others**
