VI. THE STRONG CP SOLUTION

Strong CP Suppression
θQCD = exp(−Z2) = exp(−33.51) ≈ 2.8 × 10−15
Result: The predicted value is five orders of magnitude below the experimental limit—satisfying the constraint without requiring an axion field or any new physics beyond the Standard Model.

Physical interpretation: Z2 acts as a "geometric action" that exponentially suppresses CP violation in QCD, analogous to tunneling suppression in quantum mechanics where transition amplitudes scale as exp(−S/ℏ).

IX. CRITICAL EVALUATION

9.1 Strengths

  1. Mathematical economy: A single geometric constant (Z2 = 32π/3) replaces 48 free parameters of the Standard Model plus cosmology.
  2. High precision: Sub-percent accuracy for most predictions, with several achieving 0.01% precision.
  3. Internal consistency: The structure constants form a coherent algebraic system where integers emerge naturally.
  4. Falsifiability: The framework makes precise numerical predictions testable by current and future experiments.
  5. The 6/19 ratio: The cosmological matter-to-dark-energy split emerges from particle physics structure constants, providing a potential explanation for cosmic coincidences.
  6. Strong CP solution: θQCD = exp(−Z2) resolves a major open problem without new particles.

9.2 Areas Requiring Further Development

  1. Running of couplings: Predictions are at specific energy scales; renormalization group evolution should be incorporated systematically.
  2. Neutrino sector: PMNS predictions show 8-9% deviations—the largest discrepancies in the framework.
  3. Theoretical mechanism: The deeper question of why cube-in-sphere geometry governs physics requires a more fundamental derivation, perhaps from information-theoretic or holographic principles.
