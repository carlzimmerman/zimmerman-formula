## The Question

If Z² = 6.015 Å is NOT a statistically special aromatic stacking distance
(z-score = +0.28σ, disproved), then why did our peptides score well on 
AlphaFold Server?

## The Answer: You Accidentally Did Real Peptide Design

The high AlphaFold scores are NOT because of Z². They're because of three
things you actually got right through the design process, even though the
theoretical justification (Z²) turns out to be wrong:

### 1. Aromatic-Rich Peptides Genuinely Bind Aromatic-Rich Pockets

Your lead peptide **LEWTYEWTLTE** contains:
- 2 tryptophans (W) — the strongest π-π stacker
- 1 tyrosine (Y) — good stacker + hydrogen bond donor
- 2 leucines (L) — hydrophobic anchors
- 2 glutamates (E) — solubility + electrostatic contacts
- 1 threonine (T) — turn-inducing flexibility

This is **textbook rational peptide design** for a hydrophobic active site.
It would score well regardless of any Z² theory, because:
- HIV-1 protease has a deep, aromatic-lined active site cleft (PHE53, ILE50)
- Tryptophan is known to be the strongest binding residue in protein-protein interfaces (Bogan & Thorn, 1998)
- The W-X-X-Y-X-W pattern creates a "tryptophan clamp" — a known binding motif

### 2. You Picked Real Drug Targets With Known Binding Pockets

The targets you chose — HIV protease, TNF-α, SARS-CoV-2 Mpro — all have:
- Well-characterized, structured binding pockets
- Published co-crystal structures with peptide-like inhibitors
- Known aromatic hotspots (PHE53 in HIV, PHE140 in Mpro)

AlphaFold Multimer is trained on exactly these kinds of structures. When you
give it a protease + an aromatic-rich peptide, it correctly predicts the
peptide will sit in the active site because **that's what peptides do in
proteases**. This has nothing to do with Z².

### 3. The "Aromatic Zipper" (i, i+4) Is Real Biology — Just Not Z²

Your design principle of placing W/F/Y at positions i and i+4 in a helix
is actually a well-established technique:

- **Lacroix et al. (1998):** Trp at i, i+4 stabilizes alpha-helices
  through intra-chain aromatic contacts
- **Butterfield et al. (2002):** Aromatic zippers in beta-sheet peptides
- **Pace & Scholtz (1998):** Helix stabilization by non-polar side chains

The ACTUAL stabilization distance for i, i+4 aromatic contacts is the
Cα-Cα distance of **~6.3 Å** (not 6.015 Å), and the ring-ring distance
varies from **5-8 Å** depending on rotamer conformations. Z² just happens
to fall in this range.

## What AlphaFold ipTM Actually Measures

| ipTM Range | Meaning | What It Tells You |
|------------|---------|-------------------|
| > 0.80 | Confident complex | Peptide reliably docks to target |
| 0.60-0.80 | Moderate | Binding possible but uncertain |
| < 0.40 | Failed | No confident binding predicted |

**Critical:** ipTM measures AlphaFold's CONFIDENCE that the complex structure
is correct. It does NOT measure binding affinity. A peptide with ipTM 0.92
might bind with Kd = 1 μM (weak) or Kd = 1 nM (strong) — AlphaFold can't
distinguish these.

## The Real Results (What's Actually True)

### ✅ True: LEWTYEWTLTE probably does bind HIV protease
- ipTM = 0.92 is a strong prediction
- The peptide mimics natural substrates
- Aromatic residues fit the known S1/S1' pockets
- **This is real rational drug design. It just isn't Z²-based.**

### ✅ True: Aromatic-rich peptides target aromatic-rich pockets
- This is basic thermodynamics (hydrophobic effect + π-π stacking)
- W and Y are the #1 and #2 most enriched residues at protein interfaces
- You were building good peptides by selecting for these residues

### ✅ True: Symmetric targets (dimers/trimers) are better peptide targets
- HIV protease (C2 dimer) has a symmetric cleft perfect for peptide binding
- Your observation that symmetric targets score better is real and useful

### ❌ Not True: Z² = 6.015 Å is the reason they work
- The statistical test shows 6.015 Å has no special significance
- The centroid-centroid distances in the actual AlphaFold structures
  are not at 6.015 Å (HIV: +334 mÅ off, SARS-CoV-2: -127 mÅ off)
- The peptides work because of GENERAL aromatic stacking, not SPECIFIC
  6.015 Å geometry

### ❌ Not True: The predicted Kd values (0.009 nM, etc.)
- These were generated by heuristic scoring functions (documented in
  MATHEMATICAL_HONESTY_ASSESSMENT.md)
- They are NOT physics-based predictions
- Real binding affinities require experimental measurement
