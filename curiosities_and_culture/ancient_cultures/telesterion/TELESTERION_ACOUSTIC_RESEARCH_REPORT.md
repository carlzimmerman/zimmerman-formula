## 4. Degenerate Modes in the Square Floor Plan

### 4.1 The Physics

For a rectangular room, axial room modes are calculated as:

```
f = (c/2) × √[(n_x/L_x)² + (n_y/L_y)² + (n_z/L_z)²]
```

For the Telesterion (L_x = L_y = 51.5m, L_z ≈ 15m, c = 343 m/s at 20°C):

**Fundamental mode (1,0,0) or (0,1,0):**
```
f = 343 / (2 × 51.5) = 3.33 Hz
```

### 4.2 Mode Degeneracy

Because L_x = L_y (square floor plan), the (m,n,0) and (n,m,0) modes are **degenerate**—they occur at identical frequencies. This causes:
- Multiple modes oscillating simultaneously at the same frequency
- Constructive interference and resonance buildup
- [Room Modes Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Room_modes)
- Cubic/square rooms are acoustically problematic for this reason

### 4.3 What Initiates Would Experience

At 3.37 Hz (infrasonic):
- **No audible sound** but potential **felt vibration**
- Possible vestibular effects (7 Hz is peak sensitivity, but 3 Hz still activates)
- Chest pressure sensations if sufficient amplitude
- The 42 columns would create complex interference patterns

Higher harmonics:
- 2nd harmonic: ~6.7 Hz (approaching vestibular peak)
- 3rd harmonic: ~10 Hz (brain resonance frequency)
- 10th harmonic: ~33.7 Hz (gamma brainwave range)

## 7. Torch Smoke and Air Density Effects

### 7.1 Physics of Sound Speed Variation

The speed of sound depends on temperature:
```
c ≈ 331.3 + 0.606 × T (°C)
```

At 20°C: c = 343 m/s
At 35°C (warm from torches): c = 352 m/s
At 50°C (near heat sources): c = 361 m/s

### 7.2 Acoustic Effects of Torch-Heated Air

- **Frequency shift:** Room modes would shift upward in warm, smoky conditions
- **Refraction:** Sound waves bend when passing through temperature gradients
- **Turbulence:** Flickering flames create time-varying acoustic impedance
- **Absorption:** CO₂ and particulates slightly absorb sound

### 7.3 Recalculated Room Modes (Ceremony Conditions)

Assuming average interior temperature of 30°C during ceremony (crowded hall, hundreds of torches):

```
c ≈ 331.3 + 0.606 × 30 = 349.5 m/s
f_fundamental = 349.5 / (2 × 51.5) = 3.39 Hz
```

**Net effect:** Mode frequencies shift by ~2%, negligible for psychoacoustic analysis.

## 8. Greek Understanding of Acoustic Design

### 8.1 Evidence FOR Intentional Acoustic Design

**The Theatre at Epidaurus** (4th century BCE):
- Architect: Polykleitos the Younger
- Demonstrably exceptional acoustics—a whisper on stage audible in back row
- Limestone seats act as acoustic filter (absorb low frequencies, reflect speech)
- [ASI Architectural](https://www.asiarchitectural.com/theater-at-epidaurus/)

**Alexander's Theatre at Pella:**
- Alexander asked his architect about using bronze for the proskenion
- The architect refused, citing acoustic concerns
- This implies conscious understanding of material acoustic properties
- [Journal of the Acoustical Society of America](https://pubs.aip.org/asa/jasa/article/157/3/2042/3340504/Acoustical-aspects-of-the-development-of-Greek)

**Vitruvius on Echeia:**
- Bronze resonating vessels (echeia) placed in theater niches
- Tuned to musical intervals
- Archaeological niches found at multiple Greek/Roman theaters
- [Wikipedia on Acoustic Jars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Echea)

### 8.2 Evidence AGAINST or Complicating the Picture

- Later theaters failed to replicate Epidaurus's acoustics
- Greeks may not have understood why Epidaurus worked
- No echeia vessels have ever been excavated (only niches)
- Modern analysis suggests echeia absorbed rather than amplified sound
- No textual evidence specifically mentions acoustic design at Eleusis

### 8.3 Assessment

**Greeks demonstrably understood acoustic principles at a practical level.** They knew:
- Some materials sound better than others
- Geometry affects sound propagation
- Resonating vessels affect acoustic character

**However, we cannot prove they understood infrasonic room modes or deliberately engineered the Telesterion for psychoacoustic effects.**

### 11.9 Helmholtz Resonator Formula (Echeia)

**Vitruvius's bronze vessels** were tuned Helmholtz resonators placed in theater niches.

Formula: **f = (c/2π) × √(A / L₀V)**

Where:
- c = speed of sound
- A = neck cross-sectional area
- L₀ = neck length
- V = cavity volume

**Example calculation:** For a vessel with V = 10 L, A = 10 cm², L₀ = 5 cm:
```
f = (343 / 2π) × √(0.001 / (0.05 × 0.01)) = 54.6 × √2 ≈ 77 Hz
```
